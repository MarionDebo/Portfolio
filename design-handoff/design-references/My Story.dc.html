MY STORY

Not a résumé.
A throughline.

I'm Marion, a product manager who fell for the messy middle, where user needs, business reality and what's technically possible all collide. Six years in, the throughline hasn't changed: turn complexity into something a human can actually use. Lately, that means building at the edge of AI.

The Journey

FOUR CHAPTERS · ONE DIRECTION OF TRAVEL

CH. 01 · THE SPARK

Falling for the problem

I didn't set out to manage products. I set out to understand why people abandon the very thing that was supposed to help them. Product was simply where that question lived — at the seam between empathy, business and engineering. I've been standing on that seam ever since.

CH. 02 · MISSION-DRIVEN

Entourage — product with a purpose

At Entourage I helped move a non-profit's app away from being a donation box and toward a place where people genuinely connect — events, communities, learning. 170,000 people now use it. The lesson that stuck: impact doesn't happen by accident. It has to be designed.

170,000people on the app
CH. 03 · INTO THE DATA

Toucan — making analytics human

Then came analytics. At Toucan I built a self-service dashboard editor so business users could shape their own embedded analytics — no SQL, no waiting on a data team. It pushed SQL-to-QSO conversion to 36%. I learned that the best data product is the one that disappears behind the decision it helps you make.

36%SQL-to-QSO conversion
CH. 04 · AI-NATIVE · TODAY

Betting on the conversation

Today I'm rebuilding analytics around conversation. Toucan AI lets people just ask — and get the answer, not a chart to decipher. It's the edge of AI, and it's exactly where I want to be: making powerful technology feel obvious, even kind.

Product Philosophy

There's no good product work without a solid team. I care about the craft, but I care more about the people I build it with. That's where the good decisions actually come from.

↑ THE TEAM BEHIND THE WORK

01

Start with the job, not the feature

Understand the real job someone is hiring the product to do. Features are just one possible answer to it.

02

Ship to learn

Evidence beats opinion. Build the smallest thing that teaches you something real, then let it change your mind.

03

Make the technology disappear

Especially with AI. The best experience asks nothing of the user and quietly does the hard part for them.

04

The team is the product

A clear, trusting, curious team ships better products than any roadmap ever could. Protect it fiercely.
