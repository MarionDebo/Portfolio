## Context

The platform required a 3-week professional services engagement before a customer could run a single query. This was fine for enterprise sales, but killed any self-serve or product-led motion.

## The problem

Time-to-value was entirely gated on human availability — ours and the customer's. The onboarding checklist had 47 steps. Completion rate: 12%.

## What I did

I ran a teardown of every onboarding touchpoint, shadowed 8 customer onboarding calls, and mapped every step to a "could this be a product decision?" question. 34 of the 47 steps could be automated, defaulted, or eliminated entirely.

I led a cross-functional squad (2 engineers, 1 designer, 1 data engineer) through a full redesign focused on a single north-star: **get the customer to their first meaningful query in under 2 hours**.

Key decisions I drove:
- Auto-schema detection on data source connection
- Smart defaults for transformation logic (overridable, not required)
- Progressive disclosure — hide advanced config until after first success
- In-product guided activation replacing the human-led kickoff call

## Impact

- **Time-to-value: 3 weeks → <2 days**
- **Onboarding completion rate: 12% → 71%**
- Unlocked a PLG trial motion, previously impossible
- Support tickets in week 1 dropped by 55%
